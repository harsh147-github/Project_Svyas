Every existing channel has failed
CPGRAMS

Government claims 91% disposal rate — but "disposed" doesn't mean "resolved." Satisfaction is just 62%. West Bengal resolves 1%. Complaints closed with copy-paste responses.

PMC Road Mitra

Pune's pothole-reporting app launched August 2025, received 3,904 complaints, then stopped working within six months. Both IT and Road departments were unaware it had ceased functioning.

Twitter / Reddit / Change.org

Millions of opinions daily. Zero synthesis. Zero routing to the right authority. Change.org collects signatures with no solutions. Reddit threads die in 24 hours with no output.

MyGov.in

Government asks pre-set questions. Citizens answer surveys. Top-down, not citizen-initiated. No collective intelligence. No synthesis. No accountability layer.

Voting says "I choose you."
SUAS says "Here's what we need you to do, here's how, and we're all watching."

02 — The Solution

SUAS: A National Collective Intelligence Platform

Citizens contribute casual inputs in any language. AI synthesizes them into optimal solutions. A transparent dashboard serves both citizens and decision-makers. Problems get decomposed, interconnected, and solved in parallel.

Citizen speaks their mind — zero friction

Open the app. See problems in your area. Type or speak a "vibe answer" in Hindi, Marathi, English — any language. "Kharadi roads are destroying bikes every monsoon." No formal writing needed. 5 seconds.

AI refines casual input into structured intelligence

AI converts your vibe into: Problem classification (road infrastructure), governance level (ward PWD), proposed solution (resurface 12km stretch), and feasibility estimate (₹8Cr, 3 months). You see what AI understood — approve or correct it.

AI synthesizes thousands of inputs into one optimal solution

10,000 opinions become ONE synthesized solution with weighted themes, action items, responsible authority, timeline, cost estimate, and confidence score. This is the innovation no human moderator team could replicate at scale.

Problems are mapped, interconnected, and decomposed

AI detects that "Hinjewadi traffic" is actually 4 sub-problems: missing bus routes (PMPML), narrow roads (PWD), no metro (PMRDA), and IT parks without parking (companies). Each gets assigned to the right authority, solvable in parallel.

Dashboard serves citizens AND decision-makers

Citizens see transparency: what problems exist, what solutions emerged, whether authorities responded. Officials see prioritized intelligence: a ranked queue with ready solutions, cost estimates, and one-click response that gives them public credit.

Formal petition auto-generated and routed

AI generates an official petition from the synthesis — proper letter format, RTI-ready language, routed to the exact right authority. Pothole → ward corporator. Water policy → state minister. Railway → Ministry of Railways. Response tracked publicly.

The governance hierarchy IS the architecture

Every node has a real authority. Problems auto-escalate when 5+ child nodes report the same issue.

03 — The AI Engine

Why this isn't just another community platform

Without AI, this is Reddit. With AI, this is national infrastructure. Six autonomous jobs convert noise into governance intelligence.

⬇️
Lowers the bar

Citizens don't need to write policy papers. Say "paani nahi aa raha" and AI converts it to structured intelligence.

🧮
Synthesizes at scale

10,000 citizen inputs become ONE optimal solution with themes, action items, and cost estimates. In 15 seconds.

🎯
Routes intelligently

Pothole → ward. Water policy → state. Railway → national. AI classifies governance level and routes to the right desk.

JOB 1 — REFINEgpt-4o-mini · <3 sec

"paani nahi aa raha subah se" → AI produces: Water supply disruption in Kharadi-E since morning. Category: Water. Urgency: High. Level: Ward. Auto-detected Hindi, translated, structured.

JOB 2 — ROUTEgpt-4o-mini · <2 sec

Auto-classifies every problem to the correct governance level and department. No more complaints sitting in the wrong department for weeks.

JOB 3 — SYNTHESIZEgpt-4o · 5-15 sec

Aggregates all inputs. Clusters by theme, weights by upvotes + expert verification. Generates action items with authority + timeline + cost. Tracks citizen contribution attribution.

JOB 4 — BUBBLE-UPgpt-4o · every 6 hours

Scans child locations. 5+ wards with faulty water meters → auto-creates city-level synthesis. Early warning system — crises detected before they become headlines.

JOB 5 — INTERCONNECTgpt-4o · on synthesis

Maps causal relationships between problems. "Hinjewadi traffic" decomposes into 4 sub-problems across 4 departments. Enables parallel resolution of compound crises.

JOB 6 — PETITIONgpt-4o · 5-10 sec

Generates formal petition from synthesis. Official letter format, RTI-ready language, specific authority addressing. PDF with citizen data and AI recommendations.

🏛 For Decision-Makers

A Commissioner sees "traffic complaints" as one monolithic problem for one department. SUAS decomposes it into 4 parallel workstreams: PMPML adds bus routes, PWD widens roads, PMRDA accelerates metro, companies build parking. Four authorities working simultaneously solve what one struggling alone never could.

👤 For Citizens

Citizens see WHY a problem exists — which sub-problems cause it, which authorities control each piece, and progress on each branch. When PMPML adds buses but PWD hasn't widened the road, citizens see exactly where the bottleneck is. Accountability becomes granular, not vague.

Real examples — problems that look like one but are actually many
🌊 "Kharadi Water Crisis"

Residents buying tanker water daily. Decomposition: 42% water meters faulty (Billing) + 35% pipeline leakage (Water Supply) + merged villages not connected (PMRDA) + illegal borewells (Environment). Four departments, four parallel solutions, one crisis resolved.

🏗️ "Baner-Balewadi Infrastructure Collapse"

Area grew 300% in 10 years with zero infrastructure. Decomposition: No sewage treatment (Drainage) + overloaded water supply (Water) + missing roads (PWD) + no public transport (PMPML) + illegal construction (Building Permission). Five departments, one geography.

In the stock market, a crash happens because thousands of small signals compound invisibly. In governance, a crisis happens the same way. SUAS sees the compounding in real-time and decomposes it before it becomes a crisis.
