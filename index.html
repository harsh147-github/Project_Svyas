The Gap

India votes once in 5 years.
What happens in between?

Between elections, 1.4 billion people have no structured way to tell decision-makers what they need. And decision-makers have no structured way to hear them. Both sides lose.

🏛 Officials today

Decision-makers receive thousands of complaints through CPGRAMS, Twitter, RTI, and media — all unstructured, unranked, and without solutions. They're expected to act on noise. Crises are detected from news headlines, not real-time data.

👤 Citizens today

Citizens can only file complaints — not propose solutions. There's no way to know if anyone listened, no collective voice, and no visibility. 62% find CPGRAMS inadequate. PMC Road Mitra died in 6 months.

"Sushasan isn't a complaint box or a community forum.
It's an AI-powered intelligence layer that sits between citizens and government — helping both sides make better decisions, faster."
How It Works

Three steps. That's it.

No policy papers. No formal language. No bureaucracy. Just say what you see — in any language — and AI does the rest.

🗣️
STEP 01

Citizens speak naturally

"Kharadi mein paani nahi aa raha subah se" — that's enough. Say it in Hindi, Marathi, English, or any language. Type it, speak it, or send a photo. Takes 5 seconds.

🧠
STEP 02

AI understands and synthesizes

AI converts your casual input into structured intelligence — classifies the problem, identifies the responsible authority, estimates costs, and merges it with thousands of similar inputs into one optimal solution.

🏛️
STEP 03

Officials get ready solutions

The Municipal Commissioner doesn't get another complaint — they get a prioritized dashboard with AI-synthesized solutions, cost estimates, and citizen consensus data. One click to respond publicly.

What makes this different from every other platform?
Change.org collects signatures. CPGRAMS collects complaints. Twitter creates noise.
Sushasan synthesizes intelligence. 10,000 citizen inputs become ONE actionable solution with cost estimates, responsible departments, and a timeline — in 15 seconds.

The Intelligence Layer

What the AI actually does
(in plain language)

This isn't a chatbot. It's a set of specialized AI jobs that run automatically in the background — processing, connecting, and escalating issues without anyone needing to press a button.

💬
Understands any language

Say it in Hindi, Marathi, Tamil, or English. The AI auto-detects your language and converts your casual words into a structured problem report. You review it and approve.

🎯
Routes to the right desk

A pothole goes to the ward engineer. A water policy issue goes to the state minister. The AI knows India's governance hierarchy and sends each problem to the exact right authority.

🔗
Connects related problems

"Hinjewadi traffic" isn't one problem — it's buses (PMPML), roads (PWD), metro (PMRDA), and parking (IT companies). The AI maps these connections so departments can solve them in parallel.

📊
Synthesizes thousands of voices

10,000 citizens talking about water issues become ONE optimal solution — with cost estimates, responsible department, suggested timeline, and a confidence score. In under 15 seconds.

🚨
Detects systemic patterns

If 5+ wards report the same problem, it auto-escalates to the city level. 5+ cities → state level. 5+ states → national. Decision-makers get early warnings, not late headlines.

📋
Generates official documents

When a solution has enough citizen consensus, the AI generates a formal petition with RTI-ready language, cost-benefit analysis, and a PDF — ready to submit to the relevant authority.

Built on India's actual governance structure

Every level — from your housing society to Parliament — has its own dashboard. Problems don't get lost in a single inbox. They flow through the hierarchy, escalating automatically when they're systemic.

Real Problems We're Solving

Pune is our first city.
These are real issues, verified data.

Sushasan's pilot starts in Pune with real problems that real citizens face every day. This isn't hypothetical — every problem below is sourced from official data or verified journalism.

💧 Kharadi water crisis

Residents buy tanker water daily even though they pay PMC water bills. 42% of Pune's water meters are faulty — bills are pure guesswork. AI estimates fixing this recovers ₹100Cr/year.

🚗 Hinjewadi-Baner traffic

2+ hour commute for 10km. This is actually 4 interconnected problems across 4 departments. Sushasan decomposes it so buses, roads, metro, and parking can be fixed in parallel.

📱 PMC Road Mitra failure

The city's own complaint app launched in August 2025 and stopped working within 6 months. Why? It collected complaints but had no synthesis, no prioritization, no solutions.

🏗️ Baner-Balewadi infrastructure

300% population growth in 10 years but zero infrastructure planning. Roads, water, drainage — everything is reactive. Sushasan provides data to make it proactive.

Proof that India can execute at speed:
Pune built 400km of roads in 75 days for the Bajaj Grand Tour 2026. The execution capability exists.
Sushasan provides the citizen intelligence that tells decision-makers where to direct that capability.
