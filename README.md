## The Problem

**Democracy has a signal problem.**

- **CPGRAMS** — India's official grievance system — processes 2.6M complaints a year with a 95% resolution rate. Sounds great, until you notice that's only **0.19% of India's population**.
- Meanwhile, **700M+ Indians discuss civic issues every single day** on WhatsApp, Twitter, Reddit, and Instagram. The conversation is already happening at massive scale — just not where government can act on it.
- Social media generates **outrage, not solutions.** Discussions stay scattered, no official body monitors them, and the silent majority never enters the record.

**The gap:** The way Indians naturally communicate (messy, multilingual, social) and what government can act on (structured grievances) are two disconnected worlds.

> **The capability was never the problem.**
> For the Bajaj Grand Tour (Pune, 2024) the government built 400 km of roads in 75 days and spent ₹500 Cr — because the plan was clear. When clarity exists, India delivers.
> **Sushaasan's job is to produce that clarity at national scale, bottom-up.**

## What Sushaasan Is

A social-media-style civic discussion platform that turns informal citizen conversation into structured governance intelligence.

- **For citizens:** Reddit/Instagram for civic life — anonymous, multilingual, "vibe-typing" allowed, hyperlocal by ward/city/state.
- **For AI:** Continuous synthesis of thousands of posts into topic clusters, root causes, affected populations, sentiment, and priority.
- **For leaders:** A dashboard that shows the collective problem, not 40,000 individual complaints.

**We are not replacing CPGRAMS.** We sit upstream of it — the social discussion channel that feeds structured, consensus-backed signal into the government systems that already exist.

## How It Works

```
Citizens share what they see    →    AI joins every perspective    →    Structured output    →    Visible to everyone
(any language, voice/text)           (clusters, root causes)            (dashboards + feeds)     (transparent action loop)
```

1. **Anyone contributes** — *"paani nahi aa raha subah se"* or a voice note in Marathi. 22 Indian languages, any dialect, voice or text. No signup required.
2. **Every voice joins the picture** — posts are geotagged to a ward/city and clustered with related discussions. The shy student and the loud activist count equally — we measure signal in aggregate patterns, not individual volume.
3. **AI synthesizes** — topic clustering, named entity recognition (location, department, issue), sentiment/urgency scoring, consensus detection, structured summaries.
4. **Leaders see clarity** — *"Ward 12 Pune: 40K residents discussed water supply over 2 weeks. Root cause: pipeline burst near XYZ. Suggested routing: PMC Water Dept. Estimated priority: High."*
5. **Transparency loop** — citizens see what's trending, what's been synthesized, and what action followed. Trust rebuilds when people watch discussion → synthesis → action.

## Market Research (Honest Findings)

**The good news (validated models):**
- **Decidim Barcelona** — 149K users, 71% of citizen proposals accepted
- **vTaiwan** — 4,500 citizens, 80% of consensus outputs led to government action
- **Janaagraha + MoHUA** — 2.7 Cr complaints routed via ministerial partnership
- Civic platforms **do** get government response when they have official backing.

**The hard truth (things we're planning around):**
- Government responds to *viral embarrassment* more reliably than *structured input* — we have to create institutional backing early.
- **Swachhata**: 5M downloads but 2.1★ rating (officials game the system) → moderation + transparency are core IP, not afterthoughts.
- **Koo** raised $60M and shut down → standalone social platforms are brutally hard in India.
- **LocalCircles** — sustainable, but $1.8M ARR after 12 years with 11 employees → modest civic-tech ceilings if we don't crack government integration.

**Our conclusion:** This platform succeeds or fails on **government responsiveness**, not citizen engagement. So we optimize the first pilot around *proving* synthesis → action, not chasing user counts.

## Tech Stack

| Layer | Technology | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Frontend** | React 18 PWA · Vite · TailwindCSS v4 · Framer Motion · wouter · TanStack Query | Installable, offline-capable, single codebase for mobile + desktop, consumer-grade feel |
| **Backend** | Node.js + Express · TypeScript · Drizzle ORM · PostgreSQL (Neon) · Redis (Upstash) | Type-safe, lightweight, 5-table schema (users, communities, posts, comments, synthesis_results) |
| **AI pipeline** | BullMQ (6 async jobs) — language detection & translation → topic clustering → NER → sentiment/urgency → consensus detection → structured summary | Sub-5-second civic-complaint processing, never synchronous |
| **AI providers** | **Sarvam AI** (primary — Saaras V3 ASR, Bulbul V3 TTS, function-calling) · **OpenAI GPT-4o-mini** (fallback) | Sovereign Indian LLM for government trust + data residency; OpenAI only as fallback |
| **Auth** | Firebase Phone OTP + JWT + Redis sessions · anonymous device-ID posting | Anonymous-first, identity only when needed |
| **Infrastructure** | **Vercel** (PWA) · **Railway** (API + BullMQ workers) · **Neon** (Postgres) · **Upstash** (Redis) · **Cloudflare** (CDN) | ~$20–35/month to run the whole platform for a medium city |
| **Marketing site** | **Framer** (`sushaasan.framer.website` → `sushaasan.in`) | Minimal, editable marketing surface, decoupled from the app |

**Target unit economics:** entire platform for one medium city at **~$20–35/month** (pre-government-scale).

## Integration Pathways with Government

We are deliberately designing **three parallel integration models** so we aren't single-threaded on any one government relationship:

1. **Complementary Channel** *(highest feasibility)* — position as a new citizen input channel for CPGRAMS, the way WhatsApp/IVRS/UMANG were added. Precedent: Janaagraha + MoHUA.
2. **IGMS 2.0 Analytics Extension** *(moderate feasibility)* — feed Sushaasan signal into IIT Kanpur's existing IGMS 2.0 system (already has a DARPG MoU).
3. **MyGov-style Platform** *(longer-term)* — citizen-driven bottom-up counterpart to MyGov's top-down campaigns.
