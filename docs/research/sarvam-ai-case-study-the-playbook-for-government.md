3. **Government relationships already existed**
   - Vivek: Aadhaar, Supreme Court, Bhashini advisor

   - VCs knew government contracts would follow

4. **Technical credibility**
   - PhD from ETH Zurich, Carnegie Mellon
   - Published researchers

5. **Open-source precedent**
   - AI4Bharat showed commitment to public good
   - Aligned with government's vision
   - Not just profit-seeking

**The pitch that worked:**
> "India can't build apps on foreign AI forever. We need sovereign foundational models. We're the only team in India with the experience to build this — we built Aadhaar, we built AI4Bharat, now we're building India's AI layer."

**VCs saw:** Experienced founders + national imperative + government relationships = inevitable large contracts

## How They Got Government Selection (IndiaAI Mission)

**April 2025: Selected under ₹10,372 crore IndiaAI Mission**

### The Selection Process

**What IndiaAI Mission wanted:**
- Indigenous foundational large language model
- Built from scratch in India
- Trained on Indian languages and data
- Serves Indian needs at population scale

**What Sarvam offered:**
- Team that built Aadhaar (population-scale infrastructure)
- Team that built AI4Bharat (Indian language AI)
- Already raised $41M (market validation)
- Already building models (technical proof)
- IIT Madras academic backing

**Why government selected them:**

1. **Founder credibility**
   - Vivek had government clearance (Aadhaar, Supreme Court work)
   - Pratyush's AI4Bharat was already feeding into government's Bhashini
   - Not random startup — known entities with track record

2. **Already had private funding**
   - $41M from top VCs = market validation
   - Government wasn't betting on unproven concept
   - Government was amplifying proven effort

3. **National infrastructure framing**
   - Positioned as strategic necessity (like nuclear capability)
   - "AI sovereignty" narrative aligned with government priorities
   - Not consumer product — national infrastructure

4. **Academic backing**
   - IIT Madras connection through AI4Bharat
   - Pratyush's adjunct faculty position
   - Institutional legitimacy

5. **Open-source commitment**
   - Committed to open-sourcing models under IndiaAI Mission
   - Public good alignment (not just profit)

6. **Timing**
   - IndiaAI Mission launched with ₹10,372 crore budget
   - Government needed credible teams to execute
   - Sarvam was ready with team, funding, and early models

**The government's logic:**
> "We need sovereign AI. Sarvam's founders built Aadhaar and AI4Bharat. They've already raised $41M from top VCs. They're building this anyway. Let's give them 4,000 GPUs and make it official."

**Under IndiaAI Mission selection:**

5. **Political backing**

   - National visibility
   - Signal to market: "This is India's AI champion"

**Total value:** Government support worth ₹100-200+ crore (GPU access alone)
