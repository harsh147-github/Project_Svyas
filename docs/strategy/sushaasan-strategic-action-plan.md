## Feature Design (Based on Research)

### Must-Have (Tier 1)
**Proven to drive engagement:**

1. **Civic Impact Score** (variable reward - Duolingo proof)
   - Points when your report leads to action
   - Unpredictable = addictive

2. **Weekly streaks** (not daily - civic is weekly rhythm)
   - 3x more likely to return (Duolingo data)

3. **Issue-first discovery** (TikTok's content-first model)
   - Show relevant issues based on location, not who posted

4. **Resolution tracking** (Decidim's key to success)
   - Transparent: everyone sees if government acted

5. **Reputation-gated privileges** (Reddit/Stack Overflow model)
   - Trusted users get moderation powers
   - Creates aspirational goals

### Good-to-Have (Tier 2)
6. **Consensus algorithm** (Pol.is - vTaiwan used this)
7. **Ward-level communities** (Reddit subreddit structure)
8. **Controversy dampening** (Hacker News penalty for inflammatory content)

### Iterate Later (Tier 3)
9. **Leaderboards** (Duolingo leagues)
10. **Achievement badges**
11. **Adaptive difficulty** (simple polls → complex budget trade-offs)

## Technical Reality Check

### What's Actually Feasible
✅ Topic clustering (IGMS 2.0 already does this with 85% accuracy)  
✅ Named entity recognition for routing (NextGen CPGRAMS uses this)  
✅ Multilingual support (BHASHINI APIs - government-provided)  
✅ Sentiment and urgency scoring  
✅ Structured summary generation (GPT-4/Claude-class LLMs)

### What's NOT Feasible
❌ "Neural network generates optimized solutions" ← **Stop saying this**  
❌ AI deciding "the best solution" (requires political judgment)  
❌ Fully automated policy recommendations

**Honest pitch:** "AI synthesizes discussions, identifies patterns, routes to departments. Humans make decisions."

## Biggest Risks (Be Honest)

### Risk #1: Government doesn't respond (CRITICAL)
**This is your #1 risk.** Swachhata failed here.

**Mitigation:**
- Secure FORMAL partnerships (not just "we'll look at it")
- Start with progressive municipalities (Indore has 100% SLA compliance)
- Public transparency (response rates visible)
- Integration with CPGRAMS (synthesis triggers existing SLA enforcement)

### Risk #2: Citizens don't adopt
**Mitigation:**
- Don't ask for exclusive use (complement Twitter, not replace)
- Hyperlocal focus (ward-level relevance)
- Gamification (make civic participation rewarding)
- Prove results (issues resolved faster on Sushaasan)

### Risk #3: Synthesis quality isn't good enough
**Mitigation:**
- Human-in-the-loop (don't fully automate)
- Start simple (clustering + summaries, not "optimized solutions")
- Use proven tech (IGMS 2.0 models, BHASHINI, GPT-4)
- **Doesn't need to be perfect, just better than raw grievance queues**
