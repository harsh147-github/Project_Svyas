# Project Svyas: System Context & Guidelines

## WHY: Project Purpose

Project Svyas (स्वास — "pure breath") is a global collaborative workshop where people BUILD real solutions to real problems — together. Not report them. Not discuss them. BUILD them.

Think of the final scene in 3 Idiots. Rancho's school in Ladakh. Kids inventing things. A guy who made a machine to shear sheep faster. Another who built a solar-powered bike. Nobody asked "are you qualified?" Nobody said "the government should do this." They just saw inconveniences in daily life and BUILT solutions. Real, working, tangible things.

**Svyas is that school — but it's an app, and the entire world is enrolled.**

### The Core Philosophy (READ THIS FIRST — EVERYTHING FLOWS FROM HERE)

**Svyas is NOT a complaint platform. It's NOT about reporting problems. It's NOT journaling. It's NOT social media for activism.**

**It's a place where people come together and BUILD actual working products, tools, and technology to solve problems — then implement them in real life.**

The difference:
- ❌ "This river is polluted. Someone should fix it." → That's Twitter. That's useless.
- ✅ "This river is polluted. Let's build a low-cost sensor that texts the municipality when toxin levels spike. Who knows Arduino? Who knows the local water board? Let's make this in 2 weeks." → **That's Svyas.**

- ❌ "Elderly people can't use UPI. So sad." → That's Instagram activism. Changes nothing.
- ✅ "Elderly people can't use UPI. Let's build a simplified UPI app with 3 giant buttons and voice guidance in Hindi. Here's my Figma mockup. Who can code the Android version? Let's ship it." → **That's Svyas.**

- ❌ "Street vendors lose track of their money." → Observation. Everyone knows this.
- ✅ "Street vendors lose track of their money. Let's build a WhatsApp bot where they just text their daily earnings and it auto-calculates weekly profit. No app download needed. I'll write the bot, who can test it with actual vendors?" → **That's Svyas.**

**Every "problem" on Svyas is actually a BUILD PROJECT.** The feed isn't a list of complaints. It's a showcase of things being BUILT, right now, by people who decided to stop waiting.

### The Rancho Principle: No Problem Is Stupid

In 3 Idiots, one guy built a better way to shear sheep. That's not a "world-changing" problem. It's an inconvenience. But for that farmer, it changed everything.

**On Svyas, the scale of a problem doesn't matter. What matters is:**
1. Does it cause inconvenience to someone?
2. Can we build something to fix it?

Examples of "small" problems that are PERFECT for Svyas:
- "My grandmother's medicine bottles all look the same. She mixes them up." → Let's design color-coded labels with QR codes that speak the medicine name aloud.
- "Auto-rickshaw drivers overcharge tourists in Goa." → Let's build a fare calculator app with GPS that shows the real price.
- "The water tanker comes at random times in our colony." → Let's build a simple SMS alert system with the tanker driver.
- "My school's lost-and-found is a mess." → Let's build a simple web page where people can post and claim items.

**None of these need a startup. None need funding. None need permission. They need 3-5 curious people and a weekend.**

That's Svyas.

### TWO MODES: Timepass & Deep Build

**MODE 1: TIMEPASS (Scroll, React, Contribute Small Pieces)**
- Open the app bored, like Instagram
- Scroll through a feed of things people are BUILDING
- See a project: "Building a WhatsApp bot for farmer market prices"
- Upvote it. Comment: "My uncle sells onions, he'd test this"
- See a small task: "Find 5 mandi price APIs" → takes 15 mins → do it → earn karma
- Close app. You just moved a real solution forward.
- **This is the world's most productive form of scrolling.**

**MODE 2: DEEP BUILD (Like Open Source — Serious Contribution)**
- You care about a specific build
- Enter the Build Room — full context, code, designs, data, discussions
- Claim a meaty task: "Build the Marathi language module"
- Fork the approach: "What if we use SMS instead of WhatsApp?"
- Write code, design mockups, test with real users
- Push your contribution. Community reviews. Admin merges.
- The thing you built gets used by real people.
- **This is open source for EVERYTHING, not just software.**

**Mode 1 feeds Mode 2.** The casual scroller who comments "my uncle would test this" is the connection that gets the WhatsApp bot to actual farmers. Remove either mode and solutions are weaker.

### Why This Hasn't Existed Before

Every platform makes one of these mistakes:
1. **Too much talking, not enough building** (Reddit, Twitter, OpenIDEO) — Endless discussion. Zero output. Where's the actual product?
2. **Too gated** (Solvearn, HeroX) — Need applications, approvals, budgets. By the time you're "qualified," you've lost interest.
3. **Too technical** (GitHub) — Open source is amazing but only developers participate. A farmer with domain knowledge can't open a Pull Request.
4. **Too serious** (NGOs, incubators) — You have to register an organization, write proposals, get funding. Just to solve a small problem?
5. **Too individual** (Hackathons) — 48 hours, then the project dies. No continuity. No community.

**Svyas combines:**
- The casual accessibility of Instagram (scroll, tap, contribute)
- The build culture of open source (fork, code, ship)
- The community of Reddit (anyone can participate, upvote, discuss)
- The action bias of a hackathon (BUILD something, don't just talk)
- The continuity of a real product (projects live forever, anyone can pick up where others left off)

**Into one app that works on your phone.**

### The GitHub Branch Analogy

When someone joins a problem at any stage, they can:
- **See the full history** — what's been tried, what worked, what didn't
- **Ask questions** — "Why isn't this implemented yet?" / "Has anyone tried doing this in rural India?"
- **Fork an idea** — take an approach in a different direction (like a Git branch)
- **Contribute anonymously** — drop an insight, a link, a sketch, without needing an account or identity
- **The project admin can "merge"** — pull good ideas into the main thread

This means:
- A farmer in Gujarat can see a clean water project and say "this filter design won't work with our soil type" — and that's an invaluable contribution
- A teenager can ask "why not just use SMS instead of an app?" — and that question reframes the entire approach
- A retired engineer can spend 10 minutes reviewing a technical plan and leave one comment that saves months of work

**No gatekeeping. No applications. No interviews. Just problems and curious humans.**

### Competitive Landscape & Why Svyas Wins

**Solvearn** (solvearn.net):
- Task cards for team building. You create a project, people request to join, you accept them, then collaborate.
- **Why Svyas wins**: Solvearn requires you to "apply" to join a team. It's a gatekeeper model — the project owner decides who's "good enough." Svyas has NO gates. Anyone can walk into any problem. Also, Solvearn is dead without humans online. Svyas has AI agents working 24/7.

**HeroX** (herox.com):
- Prize challenges ($5K-$1M). Organizations post problems, global community submits solutions. Judges pick winners. Takes 3-4 months.
- **Why Svyas wins**: HeroX is for organizations with budgets. A kid with an idea can't use HeroX. Svyas is free, instant, and for everyone. Also HeroX is a competition (one winner). Svyas is collaborative (everyone contributes, everyone benefits).

**OpenIDEO** (openideo.com):
- IDEO's design thinking platform. 3-5 month challenges. 200+ countries. Ideas build on each other. Community Fellows guide the process.
- **Why Svyas wins**: OpenIDEO stops at ideas — nobody builds anything. It takes months. Contributors work for free with no recognition. Svyas goes from idea to actual working prototype (AI Builder Agent writes code). Karma rewards every contribution. And we compress months into days.

**Reddit/Twitter/Instagram:**
- People already discuss problems on social media. But there's no structure. No continuity. No way to actually BUILD a solution from a viral tweet.
- **Why Svyas wins**: We take that same casual, scrollable, social energy — but add structure (stages, tasks, AI agents) so that conversations actually become solutions. Reddit is where people complain about problems. Svyas is where people solve them.

### The Svyas Advantage (What Nobody Else Has)

1. **Zero-barrier entry** — No skills required. No application. No approval. See a problem, jump in.
2. **Instagram-like discovery** — Scroll a feed of problems. Tap into what interests you. Contribute in 3 minutes or 3 hours.
3. **Any-stage joining** — Join a problem at day 1 or day 100. Full history is visible. AI catches you up.
4. **Anonymous contributions** — Fork ideas, drop insights, suggest approaches without an account.
5. **AI workforce** — Agents research, build code, write pitches while humans sleep.
6. **Problem communities** — Not teams. Communities. Open, fluid, no membership required.
7. **Karma without gatekeeping** — Earn reputation by being helpful, not by having credentials.
8. **Democratized problem-solving** — No more "only Stanford labs and Fortune 500 companies solve real problems."

## WHAT: Architecture & Tech Stack

### The Feed-First Architecture

Unlike traditional project management tools (top-down, structured), Svyas is built feed-first:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  THE FEED (What users see first)                         │
│  Scrollable problem cards — like Instagram posts          │
│  Each card: problem title, stage, contributors, activity │
│  Tap to enter → see full history, discussion, tasks      │
│  Anyone can contribute without "joining"                 │
└───────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────┘
                        │
┌───────────────────────▼─────────────────────────────────┐
│  THE PROBLEM ROOM (Where work happens)                   │
│  Discussion thread (Reddit-style, not Slack-style)       │
│  AI Agent panel (ask any agent for help)                 │
│  Task board (claim tasks, earn karma)                    │
│  Progress timeline (full history of what's been done)    │
│  Branch/Fork option (take idea in new direction)         │
└───────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────┘
                        │
┌───────────────────────▼─────────────────────────────────┐
│  THE VAULT (Supabase — stores everything)                │
│  PostgreSQL + Auth + Realtime + pgvector                 │
│  User profiles (optional — anonymous is fine)            │
│  Problems, discussions, tasks, contributions, karma      │
│  Row-Level Security for data protection                  │
└───────────────┬───────────────┬──────────────────────────┘
                │               │
┌───────────────▼───┐   ┌───────▼──────────────────────────┐
│  AI WORKFORCE     │   │  DISCOVERY ENGINE                 │
│  Claude API       │   │  pgvector (meaning-based)         │
│  6 specialized    │   │  NOT skill-matching               │
│  agents that DO   │   │  Instead: interest-matching       │
│  actual work      │   │  "Show me problems I'd care about"│
│                   │   │  Based on what you've browsed,    │
│                   │   │  contributed to, and cared about  │
└───────────────────┘   └──────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Key Difference: Discovery Engine, NOT Matching Engine

**Old concept (wrong):** "Match User A's skills to Project B's requirements"
**New concept (right):** "Show User A problems they'd find interesting based on their curiosity patterns"

This is like how Instagram's algorithm works — it doesn't match your "skills" to content. It shows you things you'd CARE about based on what you've engaged with before.

The pgvector database still powers this, but instead of embedding "skills," we embed:
- What problems has this person looked at?
- What topics do they engage with?
- What kind of contributions do they make?
- What problems are trending right now?

This turns the "matching engine" into a "discovery feed engine."

### Database Schema (Revised for Feed-First)

```sql
-- Profiles: OPTIONAL. Anonymous contributions are welcome.
profiles (
  id uuid,

  display_name text,

contributions (
  id uuid,
  problem_id uuid,

follows (
  user_id uuid,
  problem_id uuid,

-- Upvotes (like Instagram likes, but for contributions)
upvotes (
  user_id uuid,
  contribution_id uuid,
  created_at timestamptz
)

-- Karma events
reputation_events (
  id uuid,
  user_id uuid,
  event_type text,            -- 'contribution', 'task_done', 'upvote_received', etc.
  points int,
  problem_id uuid,
  created_at timestamptz
)

-- Notifications
notifications (
  id uuid,
  user_id uuid,
  title text,
  body text,
  type text,
  read boolean,
  link text,
  created_at timestamptz
)
```

## CRITICAL RULES

### Security
- Row-Level Security on ALL tables — users only see/edit what they should
- Anonymous contributions are stored without user_id — cannot be traced back
- API keys NEVER on frontend
- Karma can ONLY be created server-side (no cheating)
- Agent containers are sandboxed (Docker, ephemeral, isolated)

### Philosophy (ENFORCE IN ALL CODE AND COPY)
- **Never ask for qualifications.** No "What are your skills?" Instead: "What do you want to build?"
- **Never gate access.** No "Request to join" buttons. Every build is open.
- **Always show the build log.** Anyone joining at any stage sees full history of what's been built.
- **Always allow anonymous.** Auth is optional for browsing and small contributions.
- **Feed first.** Home screen is a FEED of things being BUILT, not a dashboard.
- **Build language everywhere.** Say "builds" not "problems." Say "Build Room" not "Project Room." Say "builders" not "members." Say "ship" not "complete."
- **Output-oriented.** Every screen should remind the user: the goal is a WORKING PRODUCT, not a discussion.
- **No problem is stupid.** The UI should never make someone feel their idea is too small. Auto-rickshaw fare calculators are as valid as cancer research tools.
- **Rancho energy.** The whole platform should feel like a lab where curious people are inventing things — messy, exciting, productive, joyful.

### Design System — FRAMER-QUALITY STANDARD

**This is critical. The website must feel like a $50,000 agency site, not a hackathon project.**

Study these Framer principles and apply ALL of them:

#### 1. SCROLL-DRIVEN STORYTELLING (Most Important)
Nothing should be visible on page load except the hero. EVERYTHING ELSE reveals as you scroll:
- **Text reveal**: Headlines animate word-by-word or line-by-line as they enter the viewport. Use `IntersectionObserver` with staggered delays. Text starts at `opacity: 0; transform: translateY(40px); filter: blur(4px)` and transitions to `opacity: 1; transform: none; filter: none`.
- **Parallax layers**: Background elements scroll at 0.3x speed, midground at 0.6x, foreground at 1x. This creates depth. Use `transform: translateY(calc(var(--scroll) * -0.3))`.
- **Sticky sections with scroll progress**: Key sections stick to viewport while content transforms within them. Use `position: sticky; top: 0` with scroll-linked animations.
- **Card stacking**: Problem cards stack on top of each other as you scroll, each one slightly scaled down and offset. Like flipping through a deck.

#### 2. MICRO-INTERACTIONS (What Makes It Feel Alive)
Every interactive element must respond to the user:
- **Hover lift**: Cards lift 8px with a soft shadow bloom: `transform: translateY(-8px); box-shadow: 0 24px 80px rgba(0,0,0,0.3), 0 0 40px rgba(52,211,153,0.06)`.
- **Button press**: Scale to 0.97 on mousedown, 1.0 on release. `transition: transform 0.1s ease`.
- **Input glow**: Focus state adds a green ring that pulses subtly: `box-shadow: 0 0 0 3px rgba(52,211,153,0.1), 0 0 40px rgba(52,211,153,0.04)`.
- **Cursor-aware tilt**: Cards tilt slightly toward the mouse position using `perspective(800px) rotateX/Y`. Subtle — max 3 degrees.
- **Magnetic buttons**: CTAs subtly pull toward the cursor when nearby (within 100px radius).

#### 3. TYPOGRAPHY (The Silent Hero)
- **Display font**: `Satoshi` or `Cabinet Grotesk` for headlines — NOT Inter, NOT DM Sans for headings. Something with character.
- **Body font**: `DM Sans` or `Plus Jakarta Sans` at 15-16px, line-height 1.7.
- **Mono font**: `JetBrains Mono` or `DM Mono` for code and technical elements.
- **Sizes**: Hero title: `clamp(48px, 8vw, 96px)`. Section titles: `clamp(32px, 5vw, 56px)`. Use `letter-spacing: -0.03em` on large text.
- **Weight contrast**: Light (300) for large text, medium (500) for body, bold (700) for emphasis. Never use all bold.
- **Gradient text**: Accent headlines use `background: linear-gradient(135deg, #34D399, #60A5FA); -webkit-background-clip: text; -webkit-text-fill-color: transparent`.

#### 4. COLOR & ATMOSPHERE
```css
/* Not just dark — ATMOSPHERIC */
--bg: #050A07;           /* Near-black with green undertone */
--surface: #0A1510;      /* Cards, panels */
--surface-hover: #0F1F17;
--border: #152A1E;       /* Barely visible, just structure */
--border-hover: #1E3D2B;

/* Accent palette — use sparingly */
--green: #34D399;        /* Primary — life, growth, go */
--green-glow: rgba(52,211,153,0.04);  /* Ambient atmosphere */
--blue: #60A5FA;         /* Secondary — trust, depth */
--amber: #FBBF24;        /* Attention, warmth */
--rose: #FB7185;         /* Energy, urgency */
--violet: #A78BFA;       /* Creativity */

/* Text hierarchy — 4 levels only */
--text-1: #EDF5F0;       /* Headlines, emphasis */
--text-2: #94AFA0;       /* Body text */
--text-3: #4A6A56;       /* Secondary, labels */
--text-4: #253D2E;       /* Barely visible, decorative */
```

#### 5. AMBIENT EFFECTS (What Makes Framer Sites Feel Expensive)
- **Gradient orbs**: Large, soft, blurred gradient circles (300-600px) floating behind content. `radial-gradient(circle, rgba(52,211,153,0.05), transparent 70%)`. Position 2-3 of them at different spots. Animate them slowly drifting.
- **Noise texture overlay**: A very subtle grain texture at 3-5% opacity over the entire page. Creates that analog, premium feel. Use SVG filter: `<feTurbulence baseFrequency="0.65" numOctaves="3" />`.
- **Dot grid or particle field**: Sparse, nearly invisible dots that connect with faint lines when near each other. The current particle canvas is good — but make particles even more subtle (0.15 opacity, not 0.25).
- **Glassmorphism for nav**: `background: rgba(5,10,7,0.6); backdrop-filter: blur(40px) saturate(1.8)`. Barely visible border.

#### 6. ANIMATION CURVES (Critical For Premium Feel)
```css
/* NEVER use linear or basic ease. Use these: */
--ease-out-expo: cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1);    /* For entrances — fast start, graceful end */
--ease-spring: cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1);   /* For bouncy interactions */
--ease-smooth: cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1);        /* For subtle transitions */

/* Durations */
--duration-fast: 0.15s;      /* Hover states, clicks */
--duration-normal: 0.3s;     /* Most transitions */
--duration-slow: 0.6s;       /* Entrances, reveals */
--duration-dramatic: 1.0s;   /* Hero animations, page transitions */
```

#### 7. LAYOUT PRINCIPLES
- **Generous whitespace**: Minimum 80px between sections on desktop, 48px on mobile. White space is not wasted space — it's breathing room.
- **Max content width**: 1200px for text, 1440px for full-bleed sections.
- **Asymmetric grids**: Not everything centered. Some sections left-aligned with offset images. Break the monotony.
- **Full-viewport hero**: The first screen should be 100vh, immersive, with a single clear message and one CTA.
- **Horizontal scroll sections**: For showcasing problem cards, use horizontal scroll with snap points on mobile.

#### 8. MOBILE-FIRST (Instagram Comparison Demands This)
- Design for 375px width FIRST, then scale up.
- Touch targets: minimum 44px.
- Bottom navigation bar (like Instagram): Home feed, Search/Explore, Create Problem, Notifications, Profile.
- Swipe gestures: Swipe between problem rooms, swipe to dismiss.
- Pull-to-refresh on the feed.
- Card-based layout that stacks naturally.

#### 9. PAGE TRANSITIONS
- When navigating between pages, content should cross-fade with a subtle slide (not a hard page reload).
- If using Next.js, use `framer-motion` for route transitions: exit animation (fade out + slide left) → enter animation (fade in + slide right).
- Duration: 0.4s with `ease-out-expo` curve.

#### 10. THE "WOW" MOMENTS (Pick 2-3 Per Page)
Every page needs 1-3 moments that make someone say "whoa":
- **Landing page**: Hero text that assembles word-by-word from blur. A problem card that floats and tilts with your cursor. The scroll-reveal of the "How it Works" pipeline with connected animated nodes.
- **Feed page**: Cards that smoothly expand when tapped. Infinite scroll that feels weightless. Pull-down particle burst on refresh.
- **Problem room**: Contributions that animate in from the side. AI agent responses that "type" in character by character. The progress timeline that fills as tasks complete.

#### REFERENCE SITES (Study These For Quality Bar)
- **antigravity.google** — Particle animations, clean typography, professional feel
- **linear.app** — Dark theme done perfectly, smooth interactions, developer-loved
- **vercel.com** — Gradient text, scroll animations, atmospheric dark design
- **raycast.com** — Command palette UX, glass morphism, premium micro-interactions
- **framer.com** — The gold standard. Every scroll, every hover, every transition is intentional.

**THE QUALITY BAR: If it looks like a template, it's not good enough. If it feels like a tool, it's not good enough. It should feel like an experience — something between Instagram and a beautiful magazine.**

## HOOK ENGINE (Critical — Read SVYAS_HOOK_ENGINE.md)

Svyas uses the EXACT same psychological mechanics that make Tinder and TikTok addictive — but pointed at building solutions instead of wasting time.

### Three Interaction Modes:

**SWIPE BUILDS (Tinder mechanic):** Full-screen build cards. Swipe right = "I want to help." Swipe left = skip. Swipe up = super like (boost visibility). "IT'S A MATCH!" when your interests align with a build's open task.

**BUILD SHORTS (TikTok/Reels mechanic):** Full-screen, swipeable progress updates. Auto-generated from build activity. Variable reward: some are celebrations, some need help, some are breakthroughs. No preview of next = compulsive swiping.

**MICRO-TASKS (Duolingo mechanic):** 2-5 minute completable tasks. Streaks ("7-day streak! Do 1 task to keep it"). Karma accumulation. Leaderboards. Completion dopamine.

### The Key Hooks:
- Variable reward loop: you don't know if next swipe is inspiring or needs your help
- Zero friction: binary choices only (swipe right/left, claim/skip)
- Instant feedback: karma +5 appears immediately, "you helped 500 students" message
- Streaks: daily contribution tracking (borrowed from Snapchat/Duolingo)
- Progress investment: once you contribute, you're invested and keep checking back
- Social proof: "47 people joined today" / "trending in India"

### The Ethical Frame:
Every addictive minute on Svyas = actual real-world impact. Same dopamine. Different output. Instead of wasting time scrolling, you're building things that help real people.
